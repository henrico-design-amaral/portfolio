# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

The primary audience is composed of tech recruiters, hiring managers, design leaders, product heads, digital-transformation teams, enterprise companies, and technology consultancies.

They visit the portfolio to assess whether Henrico Amaral can work on complex products, internal platforms, regulated environments, operational workflows, and interfaces that support real decisions.

## Product Purpose

This portfolio is a professional positioning product for Henrico Amaral, Senior Product Designer.

Its purpose is to show, with evidence and mature visual judgment, that Henrico structures real complexity. He turns ambiguous operations, fragmented information, and critical workflows into clear systems, navigable flows, and interfaces that support decisions.

The portfolio must make that understanding legible within seconds, then prove it through deep case narratives. It is not a gallery of attractive screens and should not present Henrico as a generic visual designer.

## Brand Personality

Autoral, rigorous, operational.

The voice is firm, clear, mature, specific, and restrained. It communicates authority through structure, precision, and evidence rather than slogans, hype, or visual noise.

The emotional goal is controlled confidence: the visitor should feel that complex situations are being understood, organized, and made actionable.

## Anti-references

Do not resemble:

- a generic premium SaaS landing page;
- a portfolio gallery built around isolated beautiful screens;
- a futuristic dashboard with fake data, glow, or decorative grids;
- a template that uses identical cards, badges, and micro-labels everywhere;
- an advertising page that overpromises impact or hides uncertainty;
- a lifestyle or autobiographical portfolio detached from product work;
- a visually noisy interface where texture, blur, motion, and decoration compete with the case argument.

## Design Principles

1. Structure before spectacle. Every visual decision must help the visitor understand a problem, a system, a decision, or its evidence.
2. Show complexity without performing complexity. Use real flows, relationships, screens, and constraints; avoid decorative technical theater.
3. Let evidence carry authority. Quantitative results must be traceable; qualitative results must be named as qualitative and never presented as invented KPIs.
4. Make hierarchy feel inevitable. Space, type, contrast, and sequence should direct attention before ornament does.
5. Treat interface as decision infrastructure. The visual system should connect operation, information architecture, product reasoning, and execution.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AA as the baseline. Preserve readable contrast, comfortable body text, visible focus states, semantic HTML, keyboard access, and responsive layouts across desktop, tablet, and mobile.

Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`. Content must remain visible and understandable without animation, and motion must use transform and opacity without delaying access to the case narrative.
